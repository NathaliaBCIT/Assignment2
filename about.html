I have always been creative: handwork and crafts has been always with me they have been my hobbies during all these years. After some time, I realized that the happiest parts of my life were when I let myself create something, no matter if it was painting, drawing, sewing, knitting or creating new designs for my quilts. That’s when I found out about illustration and surface design. At that moment I knew that’s what I wanted to do, and never looked back. With a real appetite for colours, shapes and patterns, my transition from my long career, of over 20 years, as an architect and interior designer to a surface designer was quite seamless.

Looking for a place where I could use my creativity and develop my passion for watercolor, I started to attend in “urban sketches” meetings, where I could bring back my observation and representation skills.

Since I am already familiarized with design software like Photoshop and Illustrator, and later Procreate as the “must-use” app, the creative work became easy and natural.

I started creating patterns with very simple shapes, using my watercolor knowledge and the Procreate app. Soon I became addicted to creating new patterns and illustrations that could be applied to apparel, home furnishings, stationery, paper products, and much more.

Currently, I have been building my portfolio while developing my personal pattern design style, taking classes, and designing lots of new patterns.
